Findr

Findr is a crowd management app my teammates and I created at DeltaHacks IV (my very first hackathon!) to enhance students studying experience. Our team’s task delegation was extremely clear as we needed to finish the project under a strict time constraint. I was in charge of doing the aesthetic design and branding of our product. Using illustrator, I designed our logo and a digital wire frame of the app with the user experience in mind. In the demo, I made sure to use no true black or whites as staring at these colors for a prolonged period of time could strain the eyes, instead, we used off whites and chose bright accent colors to provide users with a joyful greeting screen.

Weapon of choice: Illustrator, InDesign, Photoshop

Shottle

Shottle is a product designed by six undergraduate engineering student aimed to improve the "health and wellness for upper year undergraduate students". As shown in the images, the bottle has two chambers (one intended for water, the other for alcohol) of different volumes to ensure users carry enough water with them when consuming alcohol. We engineered a spring loaded lid that automatically keeps the opening accessible to the water section to encourage water consumption.

Weapon of choice: SolidWorks, 3D printing
